11 — Components

Feedback & Toasts

All feedback is warm, conversational, and at least mildly amusing. We never say "Invalid input." We say "Hmm, that doesn't quite compute — yet." Error messages are our secret weapon against dropout.

🎉

🐛
Bug spotted! Your variable ran off somewhere. Check line 3 and call it back.
🔥
Streak at risk! Come back today to keep your 7-day streak alive.
💡
Hint unlocked. You can use a for loop here — it's basically a robot doing the boring stuff for you.
Voice examples — Error messages
❌ Never say
"SyntaxError: invalid syntax"
"Undefined variable"
"Your code is wrong."
✓ Say instead
"Oops! Looks like something's off on line 3 — missing a colon? 🔍"
"Python can't find that variable — maybe it got lost? Try declaring it first 😄"
"Almost! Small thing: Python is very literal. Double-check your spelling."

✅ Body color is always blue (#0A84FF family)

✅ Pink cheek blush for cuteness, always present

❌ Never place on a light background
❌ Never use the party variant for errors

15 — Brand

Voice & Tone

We write like a brilliant, slightly chaotic best friend who happens to know Python really well. Smart but never smug. Encouraging but never condescending. Funny but never at the learner's expense.

Encouraging
Lead with what they got right. Then gently course-correct.
"So close! You got the logic spot-on — just swap those quote marks and it'll sing. 🎵"
Playful
Use metaphors from real life. Food, games, animals always work.
"Variables are just labeled boxes. Python is basically very organized storage chaos."
Direct
Cut the filler. Every word earns its place. No "Please note that…"
"Step 1: open a Python file. Step 2: type print(). Step 3: you're a programmer now."
Human
Write how people actually talk. Contractions, casual, warm.
"You're going to mess this up the first time. That's literally the whole point."
